Ramadan Daily Routine (Jessore, Bangladesh)

Weekday Routine (Monday – Saturday)
Time Activity
2:30 – 3:00 am Optional Tahajjud:
  • Engage in voluntary Tahajjud prayer and Quran recitation
3:00 – 4:00 am Suhoor & Fajr:
  • Suhoor: A nutritious meal (oatmeal, eggs, whole-grain bread, yogurt, water)
  • Fajr Prayer & brief Quran reading/dhikr
10:00 am – 9:00 pm Work from Home:
  • Begin work at 10:00 am
  • 11:00 am: 1-hour Exam Coaching session
  • Take 5–10 minute breaks hourly (incorporate brief video lesson reviews when possible)
6:30 – 7:00 pm Iftar & Maghrib:
  • Iftar: Break fast with dates, water, and a balanced meal (lean protein, vegetables, whole grains)
  • Perform Maghrib prayer after Iftar
7:00 – 9:00 pm Work Resumes:

Finish your work day while integrating brief study breaks as needed
9:00 – 10:00 pm Main Study Session:
  • Dedicate this hour to video courses and practice (to reach your 6–8 hours target)
  • JavaScript Course: Spend part of this session catching up with your JavaScript course via this YouTube link.
10:00 – 12:00 am Isha & Taraweeh at Mosque:
  • Leave for the mosque by 10:00 pm (adjust travel time as needed)
  • Perform Isha prayer and join Taraweeh (typically 10:30 pm – 12:00 am)
  • Return home after Taraweeh for rest or light review if energy permits
Sunday Routine (Weekend Variation – No Work)
Time Activity
2:30 – 3:00 am Optional Tahajjud:
  • Engage in voluntary Tahajjud prayer and Quran recitation
3:00 – 4:00 am Suhoor & Fajr:
  • Suhoor: Enjoy a healthy meal (oatmeal, eggs, fruits, water)
  • Fajr Prayer & brief Quran reading/dhikr
Flexible Morning Extended Study:

Dedicate 2–3 hours in the morning for video courses and practice (including catching up on your JavaScript course if needed)
Afternoon Afternoon Session:
  • Another 2–3 hours of focused study
  • Enjoy a light, balanced lunch (salad, lean protein, whole grains)
  • Extra spiritual/leisure time: reading, extra prayers/dhikr
6:30 – 7:00 pm Iftar & Maghrib:
  • Iftar with dates, water, and a balanced meal
  • Perform Maghrib prayer
After Iftar Evening Routine:
  • Attend mosque for Isha & Taraweeh (depart around 10:00 pm to join prayers until midnight)
  • If energy permits, spend a brief period reviewing study material or catching up on the JavaScript course
  • Relax and unwind with leisure activities

Additional Recommendations

  • Food & Hydration: Prioritize balanced meals rich in fiber, protein, and complex carbs. Stay well-hydrated during non-fasting hours and limit caffeine.
  • Exercise & Weight Loss: Incorporate short bursts of exercise throughout the day and dedicate time for a workout in the evening.
  • Spiritual Activities: Maintain daily prayers, extra Quran reading, and dhikr. Journaling your spiritual and study progress can be very beneficial.
  • Breaks & Naps: Short breaks and power naps (20–30 minutes) can help sustain energy throughout the day.
