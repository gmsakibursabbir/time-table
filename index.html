Ramadan Daily Routine (Jessore, Bangladesh)

Weekday Routine (Monday – Saturday)
Time Activity
3:30 – 4:30 am Pre-Dawn:
  • Suhoor: Enjoy a nutritious meal (oatmeal, eggs, whole-grain bread, yogurt, and plenty of water)
  • Fajr Prayer & a few minutes of Quran reading/dhikr
  • Optional light stretching or meditation
8:30 – 9:30 am Morning Preparation:
  • Wake up (if you had a short nap) and freshen up
  • Review your work tasks and plan mini study sessions
10:00 am – 9:00 pm Work from Home:
  • Start work at 10:00 am
  • 11:00 am: 1-hour Exam Coaching session for your challenging subject
  • Take 5–10 minute breaks every hour (use one break for a quick 10–15 minute video lesson review)
6:30 – 6:45 pm Iftar:
  • Break your fast with dates and water
  • Enjoy a balanced meal including lean protein (chicken, fish, or legumes), vegetables, and whole grains
Post-Iftar Maghrib Prayer:

Perform Maghrib prayer and a brief moment of spiritual reflection before resuming work
7:00 – 9:00 pm Work Resumes:

Return to work until the designated end time (adjust breaks as needed)
9:00 – 11:00 pm Main Study Session:

Dedicate 2 hours (or split into segments) to video courses and practice to achieve your 6–8 hour daily study goal.

11:00 pm – 12:00 am Evening Spiritual & Exercise:
  • Isha & Taraweeh: Perform Isha prayer and, if possible, join Taraweeh either at home or at the mosque
  • Follow with a 20–30 minute home workout (bodyweight exercises, brisk walking, or yoga)
  • Wind down for sleep
Sunday Routine (Weekend Variation – No Work)
Time Activity
Flexible Morning Morning Routine:
  • Relaxed Suhoor (or a healthy breakfast if not fasting)
  • Fajr prayer and quiet reflection
  • Extended study session: 2–3 hours of video courses and practice
  • Light exercise: a walk, yoga, or another activity for 30–60 minutes
Afternoon Afternoon Session:
  • Another study block: 2–3 hours of focused learning
  • Enjoy a light, balanced lunch (salad, lean protein, whole grains)
  • Spiritual or leisure time: extra prayers/dhikr, reading, or personal reflection
Evening Evening Routine:
  • Iftar at 6:30 pm with dates, water, and a balanced meal
  • Maghrib and Isha prayers (with Taraweeh if attending)
  • Optional short review session (around 30 minutes) or light workout
  • Relax and unwind with a hobby or leisure activity

Additional Recommendations

  • Food & Hydration: Focus on balanced meals rich in fiber, protein, and complex carbs. Drink plenty of water during non-fasting hours; limit caffeine.
  • Exercise & Weight Loss: Incorporate short bursts of exercise throughout the day and a dedicated workout session in the evening.
  • Spiritual Activities: Maintain your daily prayers, extra Quran reading, and dhikr. Consider journaling to track both your spiritual and study progress.
  • Breaks & Naps: Short breaks and power naps (20–30 minutes) can help sustain energy levels.
