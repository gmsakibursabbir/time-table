Ramadan Daily Routine (Jessore, Bangladesh)

Weekday Routine (Monday – Saturday)
Time Activity
10:00 – 12:00 am Isha & Taraweeh at Mosque:
  • Depart for the mosque by 10:00 pm
  • Isha prayer and Taraweeh (typically 10:30 pm – 12:00 am)
12:00 – 2:30 am Sleep:

Return home and get restorative sleep. If possible, try to make this a quiet, uninterrupted rest.

2:30 – 3:00 am Optional Tahajjud:
  • Wake briefly for voluntary Tahajjud prayer and Quran recitation
  • Then, if needed, a short nap before Suhoor
3:00 – 4:00 am Suhoor & Fajr:
  • Have a nutritious Suhoor (oatmeal, eggs, whole-grain bread, yogurt, water)
  • Perform Fajr prayer and a brief Quran/dhikr session
10:00 am – 9:00 pm Work from Home:
  • Begin work at 10:00 am
  • 11:00 am: 1-hour Exam Coaching session
  • Take 5–10 minute breaks hourly and incorporate brief video lesson reviews as needed
6:30 – 7:00 pm Iftar & Maghrib:
  • Iftar: Break fast with dates, water, and a balanced meal (lean protein, vegetables, whole grains)
  • Perform Maghrib prayer after Iftar
7:00 – 9:00 pm Work Resumes:

Continue and finish your work day, with intermittent study breaks as needed.

9:00 – 10:00 pm Main Study Session:
  • Dedicate this hour to video courses and practice (aim to reach 6–8 hours daily)
  • JavaScript Course: Catch up on your JavaScript course via this YouTube link
Sunday Routine (Weekend Variation – No Work)
Time Activity
10:00 – 12:00 am Isha & Taraweeh at Mosque:
  • Depart for the mosque by 10:00 pm
  • Isha prayer and Taraweeh (typically 10:30 pm – 12:00 am)
12:00 – 2:30 am Sleep:

Get restorative sleep after returning home.

2:30 – 3:00 am Optional Tahajjud:
  • Wake briefly for voluntary Tahajjud prayer and Quran recitation
  • Then, if needed, a short nap before Suhoor
3:00 – 4:00 am Suhoor & Fajr:
  • Suhoor: Enjoy a healthy meal (oatmeal, eggs, fruits, water)
  • Fajr Prayer & brief Quran/dhikr session
Flexible Morning Extended Study:

Dedicate 2–3 hours in the morning for video courses and practice (including catching up on your JavaScript course if needed)

Afternoon Afternoon Session:
  • Another 2–3 hours of focused study
  • Enjoy a light, balanced lunch (salad, lean protein, whole grains)
  • Extra spiritual/leisure time: extra prayers/dhikr, reading, or personal reflection
6:30 – 7:00 pm Iftar & Maghrib:
  • Iftar with dates, water, and a balanced meal
  • Perform Maghrib prayer
After Iftar Evening Routine:
  • Attend mosque for Isha & Taraweeh (depart around 10:00 pm to join prayers until midnight)
  • If energy permits, review study material or catch up on the JavaScript course after returning home
  • Relax and unwind with leisure activities

Additional Recommendations

  • Food & Hydration: Prioritize balanced meals rich in fiber, protein, and complex carbs. Stay well-hydrated during non-fasting hours and limit caffeine.
  • Exercise & Weight Loss: Incorporate short bursts of exercise throughout the day and dedicate time for an evening workout.
  • Spiritual Activities: Maintain daily prayers, extra Quran reading, and dhikr. Journaling your progress can be very beneficial.
  • Breaks & Naps: Use short breaks and power naps (20–30 minutes) to sustain energy levels.
